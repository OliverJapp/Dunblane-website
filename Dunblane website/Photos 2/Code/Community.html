Dunlane train station was established in 1848 and has three platforms

It has three platforms, one which serves as a terminus for trains from Glasgow (Queen Street) and Edinburgh, one which serves trains heading north to Perth, Dundee, Aberdeen and Inverness and the third that serves trains heading south to Glasgow and Edinburgh

The Dunblane Centre is a purpose-built youth, family, arts, sports and community centre.

It was built after a community vote chose that option for money from a consolidation of several funds which were created in the aftermath of the 1996 tragedy.

It opened in 2004 and receives no state finance, relying on user revenue and fundraising


The Centre allows for community activity, and allows High schoolers to volunteer
